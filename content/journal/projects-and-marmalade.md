title: Projects and Marmalade
date: 2021-11-26T10:49:38Z

Brr, okay...

So, yesterday I said that I had this intention to create a project management tool and that this used to be part of the intentions behind Marmalade... which, yeah, it totally was. Marmalade was originally conceived of as a project management tool; I initially called it Caffeine, then Coffee and at one point Cola, then Panther, and finally settled on the name Marmalade. You can see the initial focus on caffeine and caffeinated beverages - _energize your workflow_ was a slogan in consideration. The scope of the project grew and... I realised this could have personal use, family use, it could trace a person's finances and relationships, a business' inventory or even just the contents of your fridge. The scope blew up!

I mention this because... well, here's the thing, I accidentally happened across an app yesterday afternoon called [ClickUp](https://clickup.com/). I'm not recommending the app. In fact, if it looks appealing to you, I can mention a very similar alternative called [Airtable](https://www.airtable.com/) which I've known about for a while longer. They both do this really interesting thing which is... essentially... they allow you, the user, to create your own database and your own application space around that data with custom views. There's the standard data table view, they both have calendar and kanban views, timelines and custom forms. They both kind of bill themselves as project management tools, and in fact...

ClickUp have the slogan, "One app to replace them all."

Airtable lead with, "Connect everything. Achieve anything."

They both do... quote-unquote, "everything". Something which Marmalade was once criticised for doing. Constructive criticism, yes, but I believed in that model then and... I do feel partially vindicated by the success of others. That said...

Marmalade wasn't half as cool. I didn't really have these custom views. Instead, each project space had a constrained set of views. They could be activated and deactivated in some cases... but they were a little more restrictive. Still, that's... that is actually the beginning of realising what both of these apps have.

But whereas Marmalade had a pre-defined data schema that only grew as and when I updated it... these applications essentially permit the user to create their own schema on the fly. It's something I started considering for Marmalade back in the day, but uhmm... well I took a setback on that project after pitching the minimum viable version.

I'm thinking, though... This is what Marmalade should be. I can totally catch up with both of these applications in terms of feature-set. I can potentially even outpace them (in terms of feature offerings) with some of the extended features I had already written for the original version of Marmalade.

I just have to ask myself... what do I lose by doing this? Part of what made Marmalade work is that I knew the schema, and I had predetermined the relationships between things like... businesses and their investors and owners, their employees and contractors, families and households, etc. Marmalade was capable of modeling a sophisticated network of organisational spaces which... neither of these apps is capable of.

So I wonder... would each of those things... be a space? And could spaces be connected to one another with configurable relationships? Let's imagine a single chain...

- My Family
- Me
- My Limited Company
- A Division of My Limited Company
- Some Project

Each of those spaces would have... its own data views - perhaps a calendar, perhaps finances, perhaps a personnel phone book - and some of that data would have to echo up the chain of spaces. Say that "Some Project" needs my attention on some specific date, that's a calendar event that should echo up to even my family's calendar so that we in my household know I'm busy that day. Say that "A Division of My Limited Company" needs to be allocated funds for a project; this affects "My Limited Company" in terms of its financial holdings, which in turn affects the value of my ownership stake.

This is how Marmalade quickly swelled into... so much more than a project management tool. Perhaps far too much more than one.

But the point is... I can imagine that still totally working. If I could say, look... this data table in this space represents _this thing_, and that should echo onto _this value_ in that related space.

It's just... a lot more confusing and challenging for a user to arrange than Marmalade ever made it originally.

So I dunno... I think the "related spaces" concept needs to be shelved for the time being. Focus on the project management aspects. Understand that each "view" in one of these spaces is technically an application in and of itself. And... having that in mind, we can create separate integrations for this core project in the future. Y'know, like... if you get started with a table view to track finances, maybe we someways down the line introduce an accounting application that brings with it an accounting view... you could convert to the new, more restrictive but feature-rich view if you choose.

Something like that. I think it works. But focus on the core, project management part for the time being. And that's it...

I am decided. _Marmalade_ is the name of my project management tool. It's making a comeback!
