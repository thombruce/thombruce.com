...and like... well, no. Let me rephrase. I don't actually suck at branding... or titling things, as I was going to say... but I do get hung up on those things.

Take this one story I've been wanting to write for somewhere near a decade... I've still barely made any kind of a start on it, though it does see some development here and there... largely because every time I do sit down to write anything, I first go, "all right, what's the title..." and I've got nothing. Some things are easy to title. I wrote a piece called _A Silent Musical_ in university; that story changed over and over but the title was a constant from the beginning - the title was the first thing, a sort of challenge to myself. But this other story? Nothing I'm keen on in ten years...

Same goes for software development. Sometimes I get hung up on this need for a name, a brand, a title...

This is something I'm mentioning because I want to correct that. I want to be able to just go... _look, I want to make this "sort of calendar thing", let's call it... some-sort-of-calendar-thing, or just 'calendar'_... right? Because a name... what's in a name? It doesn't matter, not early on anyways. I don't actually _need_ titles, I need _working titles_.

That said, this does play into something I have just set up called [Unlabel](https://github.com/getunlabel). That is something I've wanted to do for a while; a sort of brand for a collection of apps that are open source and white label, meaning anyone can use and adapt them with their own branding. So say... I want to create a CMS (and I do), I can simply created some project like "Unlabel CMS" - a label-free app. And I could do that for a bunch of projects. It isn't... Look, it isn't for projects I simply can't think of a name for, but it does get the ball rolling on some of those, since it covers a fair bit of the stuff I've been wanting to do... a white-label CMS, hosting application, social tools... I've a few ideas for over there.

And I feel a bit freed up by that. Because I can now... and let's take a unnamed calendar project as a for instance... I can now create an app at @thombruce/calendar, unnamed and in development, and if that reachers a point where I think... _okay, hey, this works as a white label product_, then I can move it over to @unlabel/calendar and it won't need a name or brand of its own - it can just be 'Unlabel Calendar'.

I'm rambling, I apologise... I just feel liberated by this. I'm pretty tired right now, so I'm not doing much more with that today. But to simply have a place to put... nameless things. It feels good.

I just have to work on my hesitancy to start untitled writing projects now.
